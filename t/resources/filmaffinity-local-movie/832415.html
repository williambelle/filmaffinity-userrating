Drive
ORIGINAL TITLE Drive
YEAR
2011 Apple Trailer
RUNNING TIME
100 min. Suggest trailer/video
COUNTRY
(New) Visual section
DIRECTOR Nicolas Winding Refn
SCREENWRITER Hossein Amini (Novel: James Sallis)
COMPOSER Cliff Martinez
CINEMATOGRAPHER Newton Thomas Sigel
CAST Ryan Gosling, Carey Mulligan, Albert Brooks, Ron Perlman, Bryan Cranston, Oscar Isaac, Christina Hendricks, Tina Huang, Joe Pingue, James Biberi, Kaden Leos
STUDIO/PRODUCER FilmDistrict / Bold Films / Odd Lot Entertainment / Marc Platt Productions / Seed Productions
OFFICIAL WEB http://www.drive-movie.com/
AWARDS 2011: Academy Awards: Nominated for Best Sound
2011: Cannes Film Festival: Best Director
2011: BAFTA Awards: 4 nominations, including Best Film, Director & Editing
2011: Golden Globes: Nominated for Best Supporting Actor (Albert Brooks)
2011: Critics Choice Awards: Best Action Movie. 8 nominations
2011: NBR (National Board of Review): Top Ten Films of the Year
2011: Círculo de críticos de Nueva York: Best Supporting Actor (Albert Brooks)
2011: Satellite Awards: 4 awards, including Best Director. 8 nominations, including Best Picture
2011: Independent Spirit Awards: 4 nominations, including Best Picture and Direction
2011: Los Angeles Film Critics Association: Best Music
2011: Cesar Awards: Nominated for Best Foreign Film
2011: Nominated for David di Donatello: Best Foreign Film
GENRE Thriller. Action. Drama. Film-Noir | Neo-noir. Heist Film. Cult movie. Independent Film (USA)
SYNOPSIS/PLOT A mysterious man who has multiple jobs as a garage mechanic, a Hollywood stuntman and a getaway driver seems to be trying to escape his shady past as he falls for his neighbor - whose husband is in prison and who's looking after her child alone. Meanwhile, his garage mechanic boss is trying to set up a race team using gangland money, which implicates our driver as he is to be used as the race team's main driver. Our hero gets more than he bargained for when he meets the man who is married to the woman he loves.
PRO REVIEWS ----------------------------------------
"Drive" is somber, slick and earnest, and also a prisoner of its own emptiness, substituting moods for emotions and borrowed style for real audacity" (A. O. Scott: The New York Times)
----------------------------------------
"A shamelessly entertaining genre movie from director Nicolas Winding Refn that plays like an exalted episode of Miami Vice (...) It's not art, but arty mainstream (...) Rating: *** (out of four)" (Carrie Rickey: Philadelphia Inquirer)
----------------------------------------
Buckle up for the existential bloodbath of Drive, a brilliant piece of business that races on a B-movie track until it switches to the dizzying fuel of undiluted creativity (...) Rating: **** (out of four)" (Peter Travers: Rolling Stone)
----------------------------------------
Here is still another illustration of the old Hollywood noir principle that a movie lives its life not through its hero, but within its shadows (...) Rating: ***1/2 (out of four)" (Roger Ebert: Chicago Sun-Times)
----------------------------------------
"A film in love with both traditional noir mythology and ultra-modern violence, a combination that is not ideal (...) "Drive" doesn't spend a lot of time on mayhem, but what does get put on screen is intense, unsettling and increasingly grotesque" (Kenneth Turan: Los Angeles Times)
----------------------------------------
"Screw Oscar, which will surely ignore Drive because it's too bloody, too creative (...) the year's best movie. (...) Drive is pure cinema. I couldn't have liked it more." (Peter Travers: Rolling Stone)
