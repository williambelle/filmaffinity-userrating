Untouchable
ORIGINAL TITLE Intouchables
YEAR
2011 Apple Trailer
RUNNING TIME
109 min. Watch Trailers/Videos
COUNTRY
(New) Visual section
DIRECTOR Olivier Nakache, Eric Toledano
SCREENWRITER Olivier Nakache, Eric Toledano
COMPOSER Ludovico Einaudi
CINEMATOGRAPHER Mathieu Vadepied
CAST François Cluzet, Omar Sy, Anne Le Ny, Audrey Fleurot, Clotilde Mollet, Joséphine de Meaux, Alba Gaia Bellugi, Cyril Mendy, Christian Ameri, Marie-Laure Descoureaux, Gregoire Oestermann
STUDIO/PRODUCER Quad Productions / Gaumont / TF1 Films / Ten Films / Chaocorp production / Canal + / Cinecinema
OFFICIAL WEB http://www.toledano-nakache.com/
AWARDS 2012: Golden Globes: Nominated to Best Foreign Language Film
2011: Cesar Awards: Best Actor (Omar Sy). 9 nominations, including Best Film
2012: BAFTA Awards: Nominated for Best Foreign Languaje Film
2012: Goya Awards: Best European Film
2011: Tokyo International Film Festival: Best Film, Best Actor (François Cluzet & Omar Sy)
2011: Nominated for David Di Donatello: Best European Union Film
2012: European Film Awards: 4 nominations, including Best Film
2012: Satellite Awards: Best Foreign Film (ex-aequo, with "Pietà)
GENRE Comedy. Drama | Comedy-Drama. Based on a true story. Friendship. Disability
SYNOPSIS/PLOT After a paragliding accident, Philippe, a rich aristocrat, is confined to his home. He employs Driss as home help. Driss is a young guy from the projects recently out of prison. In short, the person the least adapted for the job. Vivaldi and Earth, Wind and Fire, fine language and slang, suits and jogging outfits come together and a clash is inevitable. Two worlds collide and win each other over to give birth to a friendship as crazy, funny, and fierce as it is unexpected. A unique relationship that will make sparks fly and render Philippe and Driss untouchable.
PRO REVIEWS ----------------------------------------
The plot of the film is inspired by a true story discovered by the directors in a 2004 documentary film. As of 25 November 2011, after 4 weeks, "Intouchables" has already become the film the most seen in France in 2011 and the 1st in French box office history. On March 2012 The Intouchables became the highest-grossing movie in another language than English with 281 million US$. It broke the previous record set by the Japanese film Spirited Away (274,9 million). (From wikipedia) (FILMAFFINITY)
